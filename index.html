I am an organic/organometallic chemist with expertise in synthetic methodology, mechanistic analysis, and catalysis. My research harnesses base-metal catalysis to build practical chemical tools that accelerate drug discovery.

At Princeton University, I lead an active industry–academic collaboration with Bristol Myers Squibb, successfully translating cutting-edge methodologies into industrial-applicable protocols. I have experience in managing multidisciplinary research teams consisting of postdoctoral scientists, graduate and undergraduates students across different programs.

My technical toolkit spans multidimensional NMR and LC-MS to cyclic voltammetry and photophysical characterization, underpinned by rigorous glovebox and Schlenk line expertise. I am equally comfortable leading science and building the infrastructure — laboratory setup, safety operations, and team culture — that makes great science possible.
